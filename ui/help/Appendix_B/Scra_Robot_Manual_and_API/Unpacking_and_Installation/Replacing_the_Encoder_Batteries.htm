Replacing the Encoder Batteries

The IR-820's encoders are backed up by three "C"-size alkaline batteries, located under a rectangular hatch on the top deck of the robot. In order to ensure maximum lifetime of these batteries, they should be replaced upon installing and powering up the robot.

  1. With the robot powered up, ensure that the robot's batteries are in place. These are located under a rectangular hatch on the top deck of the robot. Turn the screw a quarter turn to release the hatch and expose the battery box.

  2. Remove the battery box.

  3. Replace the batteries with three, fresh "C"-size alkaline batteries. Be sure to include the aluminum battery spacer. So not use four batteries!

 

WARNING: Do not mix old and new batteries. Use only fresh batteries from the same package.

 

Note: The batteries are standard alkaline cells. While the robot is powered, no current is being drawn from these batteries, and their expected lifetime is the same as their advertised shelf life. This is typically up to five year, but may be different depending one the brand of battery chosen. See section 8 for additional information.

 

  1. Replace the battery box.

  2. Replace the battery box cover.

  3. Turn the battery box cover screw a quarter turn clockwise to secure the cover.
