Leveling the Robot

In general, all of the devices (cassettes, FOUPs, pre-aligners, tool chucks, etc.) in the work cell should be as level as possible. Before attempting to level the robot, first level all of the devices in the work cell associated with wafer transfer. The robot should be leveled to the environment it is in, not necessarily to absolute level. The tool itself may not be level, and leveling to an absolute may result in planarity errors within the tool. The best starting point is the level of the tool's FOUPs or cassettes. Determine their level, and level the robot to be the same. If the FOUPs or cassettes are not leveled identically, then find a compromise between the two.

 

Performing this procedure will require the following tools:

  • IRSI Leveling Jig, parts number 01001.

  • 4mm Allen wrench for the mounting screws.

  • 3mm Allen wrench for the leveling screws.

 

Verify that the robot-mounting flange has the three M6 leveling screws installed (Figure 3 - Leveling Screw Locations1 - Leveling screw Locations). These go in the three threaded holes along the outer edge of the flange.
