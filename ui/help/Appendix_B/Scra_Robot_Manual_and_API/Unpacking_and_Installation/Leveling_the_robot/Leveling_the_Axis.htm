Leveling the Axis

Leveling Axis One

  1. Using one of the leveling screws along Axis one, adjust the robot until the level along Axis One is centered.

  2. Loosen the leveling jig.

  3. Leaving the leveling jig in its original position, rotate the robot shoulder 180 degree.

 

WARNING: Do not allow fingers or clothing to be caught between the robot arm links or between the arm links and the end effector! Move the robot arm with the palm of the hand, with the fingers extended. Move the elbow and wrist as needed to avoid obstacles within the tool.

 

  1. Tighten the leveling jig, maintaining its original orientation. (Compare Figure 5 - Aligning the Leveling Jig, and Figure 6 - After Rotating the Arm4 - After Rotating the Arm.)

  2. If the level reading is still in the center, skip to Leveling Axis Two.

  3. If the level reading is no longer in the center, adjust the leveling screw adjusted in step 1 until the bubble is halfway between its current location and the centered position. Make note of this new bubble position.

  4. Loosen the leveling jig.

  5. Rotate the robot shoulder back 180 degree, so it is in its original position from step 1, again leaving the leveling jig in it's original orientation.

 

Figure 6 - After Rotating the Arm

 

The bubble should be in location opposite that attained at the end of step 6, so the two readings' average is at the center of the level. If it is not, repeat step 1-8 until it is.

 

Leveling Axis Two

  1. Using the leveling screw at the end of Axis Two, adjust the robot until the level along Axis Two is centered.

  2. Loosen the leveling jig.

  3. Leaving the leveling jig in its original position, rotate the robot shoulder 180 degree.

 

WARNING: Do not allow fingers or clothing to be caught between the robot arm links or between the arm links and the end effector! Move the robot arm with the palm of the hand, with the fingers extended. Move the elbow and wrist as needed to avoid obstacles within the tool.

 

  1. Tighten the leveling jig.

  2. If the level reading is still in the center, skip to "Touching up".

  3. If the level reading is no longer in the center, adjust the leveling screw adjusted in step 1 until the bubble is halfway between its current location and the centered position. Make note of this new bubble position.

  4. Loosen the leveling.

  5. Rotate the robot shoulder back 180 degree, so it is in its original position from step 1, again leaving the leveling jig in its original orientation.

  6. The bubble should be in a location opposite that attained at the end of step 6, so the two readings' average is at the center of the level. If it is not, repeat steps 1-8 until it is.

 

Touching up

After leveling Axis Two, Axis One may require minor adjustment. Repeat the steps for leveling Axis One and Axis Two. Far less adjustment should be required for this final step.
