Jogging the Robot

Using the teach pendant, the operator may jog (move) the robot under manual control. The robot may be jogged in any of four modes: Joint, Tool, R-theta, and World. See Appendix A for complete discussion of jog modes.

 

WARNING: To avoid possible injury to personnel or equipment, always keep the robot's speed at or below 10% while under manual control. IRSI recommends setting the robot's speed at or below 5% until you are familiar with the robot's motion.

 

To jog the robot:

  1. On the teach pendant, press the "RESET" key to servo the robot. The blue "Robot Power" light will come on on the front of the controller.

  2. On the teach pendant, press the "-%" and "+%"keys as needed to reduce the speed limit (indicated on the teach pendant display) to below 5%.

  3. Press the "COORD" key as needed to select the desired jog mode. For first time operation, "Join" mode is probably the most appropriate.

  4. If the teach pendant is equipped with a liveman switch, squeeze it until it reaches a point midway through its available travel.

  5. Press and hold the "JOG" key.

  6. Press the blue movement keys on the right side of the teach pendant to move the robot as desired.

 

See Appendix B, "Jog Modes and Reference Frames" for a complete discussion of jogging.

 

Note: If for any reason there should be an error while attempting to jog, press the "RESET" button on the teach pendant and retry the operation.

 

Note: If the host has control (indicated on the upper left of the teach pendant display), issue the command "PASS CONTROL" from the host to release control to enable the teach pendant to take control. In order to execute motion control commands from the host, press the "PASS CONTROL" button on the teach pendant to make control available to the host.
