Mapping Out a Strategy

The first step in optimizing an application is mapping out a strategy. You will save a lot of work by planning the station database on paper before teaching the robot.

 

In this section, we will go through the basic steps of planning your station database.

Start with layout drawing, and ask the following questions:

  • What are the primary stations? (Ignore vias or waypoints for now)

  • What sequencing is required by the application? What are the primary paths the payloads are expected to take?

  • Where are the wait points/bottlenecks? Some examples include process duration, prealigner time, and waiting for PDOs or loadlocks to open and close.

 

The answers to these questions can help you determine approximate station locations and paths to be taken. Draw a chart showing station combinations and planned paths between each. Note that some paths may not include a payload, increasing move flexibility for the move. (I.e., the end effector may be able to get through a particular path only when no payload is present. This may be necessary between transfers, but should be handled carefully.)

 

Pay careful attention to the following limitations:

  • Each of the theta joints is limited to +/-320 degrees (640 degrees total rotation), so at some point in the cycle, you will have to plan for an "unwrap" move.

  • Linear motions can only performed in situations where the wrist does not cross over the shoulder unless the station parameter "rtheta" is set to "true".
