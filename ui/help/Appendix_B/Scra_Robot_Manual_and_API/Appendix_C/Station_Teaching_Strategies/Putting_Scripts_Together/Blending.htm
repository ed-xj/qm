Blending

The first motion arguments to be applied are the blend arguments. Blend arguments give the robot its smoothest motions by blending moves into the moves that follow them, effectively by accelerating into the following move while the previous move is still decelerating. In this way, the robot doesn't come to a complete stop between moves, which keeps cycle times to a minimum.

 

The most common blend argument is "blend 100", used like this:

move via3 blend 100

move pdo1

This instructs the robot to move to the station called "via3", but as the deceleration to "via3" begins, to begin the acceleration for the move to "pdo1". Without the blend command, the robot will come to a stop at via3 before beginning the move to "pdo1". The "100" argument is the percentage of overlap between the deceleration of the first move and the acceleration of the second move.

 

Sometimes, when a large move is followed by a very small move, standard blending is not enough. In this case, the use of a leading "F" before the percentage argument allows the smaller move to be partially or completely subsumed by the larger movement (depending on the percentage specified).

 

An example:

move via3 blend F75

 

For pick and place moves, it is better to specify which portion of the move is supposed to incorporate the blend. Use the pblend (blend the move to the perch to the move inside), iblend (blend the move inside to the stroke), blend (blend the stroke to the move back to the perch), and blend (blend the move to the perch to the next move) arguments to achieve the desired results.

 

Note that the path taken by the robot under the control of blend arguments is not speed-dependent.
