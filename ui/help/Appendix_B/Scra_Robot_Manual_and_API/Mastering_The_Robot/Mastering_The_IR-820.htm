Mastering The IR-820

Mastering and calibrating the robot establishes with the controller a correlation between servo encoder position and the robot's hard and soft stops. This process begins with moving the robot to a known position, and then telling the controller that it has arrived there.

 

Note: The robot is mastered at the factory prior to shipment. Mastering will not have to be done unless the batteries have come loose during shipment, or if the robot has been stored for an extended period. The robot does not need to be remastered during the course of normal operations.

 

Prepare for mastering:

  1. Lower the Z-axis slowly until you hit the hard stop. You should just be able to see light between the robot's aft arm and its top deck.

 

Note: When the robot actually arrives at the hard stop, you may receive a "current excess" alarm. If you receive this alarm, press the "RESET" button to clear it.

 

  1. Press the "E-stop" button to de-servo the robot.

  2. Gently rotate the aft arm until it hits the hard stop.

  3. Gently rotate the aft arm in the opposite direction about 360 degree until it faces directly away from the battery compartment lid on the top flange of the robot. This is the aft arm's approximate center position, as the arm can move about 720 degree total.

  4. Gently rotate the forearm until it hits the hard stop.

  5. Gently rotate the forearm in the opposite direction about 360 degree until it points toward the battery compartment. This is the forearm approximate center position.

  6. Gently rotate the wrist until it hits the hard stop.

  7. Gently rotate the wrist in the opposite direction about 360 degree until it points directly away from the battery compartment. This is the wrist's approximate center position.

 

Mastering:

  1. Insert the mastering peg into the hole provided in the top deck.

  2. The mastering position of the robot is achieved when all three arms touch the mastering peg on the side opposite the battery box. Press the "E-stop" button and move all of the arms manually counter clockwise close to the mastering peg, so that as each arm is being backed onto the mastering peg, the arm above it will not hit first.

  3. Place the teach pendant in "JOINT" mode by pressing the "COORD" button until "JOINT" appears on the teach pendant display.

  4. Very slowly jog the aft arm at 1% speed counterclockwise until it just touches the mastering peg.

 

Note: The arm may touch the raised ring on the mastering ped first, or it may touch the recessed area first. Whichever touches first, that is the correct position.

 

  1. Slowly jog the forearm counterclockwise until it just touches the mastering peg.

  2. Slowly jog the wrist counterclockwise until it just touches the mastering peg.

  3. From the teach pendant, press the "pass control" button.

  4. From the host communication program, issue the command "master".

  5. Waiting six seconds for the mastering to complete.

  6. From the host communication program, issue the command "reset".

 

Finishing up:

  1. From the host communication program, issue the command "pass control".

  2. Using the teach pendant "-Y" button (which moves the forearm clockwise), back the arms off the mastering peg.

  3. Press the "E-stop" button to de-servo the robot.

  4. Unscrew the mastering peg.

 

The robot is now mastered and ready to use.
