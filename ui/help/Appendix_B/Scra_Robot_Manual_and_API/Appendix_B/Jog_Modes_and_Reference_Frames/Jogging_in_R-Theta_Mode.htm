Jogging in R-Theta Mode

In R-Theta mode, the robot behaves like a standard R-Theta robot. In an R-Theta robot, the orientation of the end-effector is fixed, always pointing along a radial from the center of the robot. (Even in R-Theta mode, the IR-820's the wrist is articulated and the end-effector can be rotated into non-radial positions. This allows greater flexibility for station training and station mapping.)

 

To put the robot into R-Theta jog mode, press the "Jog Mode" button until RTHETA is displayed on the teach pendant. The R-Theta jog mode keys act as follows:

Note that while the end-effector can be rotated into non-radial positions, the radial motion of the robot will always still move the wrist (end-effector rotation point) in a line with the center of the robot (a radial). To jog the robot along the end-effector axis, regardless of its orientation, use "Tool" mode (below).

 

To put the robot into a standard R-Theta position, use the J4 jog keys to rotate the wrist to a radial orientation. The wrist will automatically stop rotating as soon as the end-effector is either pointing directly towards or directly away from the center of the robot. After putting the robot in this configuration, rotate the Theta around the desired range of travel to insure that the wrist does not encounter a soft-stop and need to be re-oriented.
