Robot Mechanical Unit (RMU)

The IR-820 robot incorporates a SCARA (Selective Compliance Articulated Robot Arm) design, in contrast to conventional Radial-Theta arm concept. The SCARA platform offers maximum wafer maneuverability that allows functional advantages like in-line wafer pick-up and hand-off, continuous trajectory from wafer pick-up to wafer hand-off, and minimum swap time with a two handed end effector.

 

The shoulder, elbow, and wrist joints can rotate +/- 320 degree. The robot is optimized for handling 200mm and 300mm wafers.

 

The IR-820 robot mechanical unit is a four-axis system that utilizes four DC brushless motors. The drives consist of the motors, harmonic drive, belts and bearings. The Z-axis and each arm joint are driven independently. A housing mounted on linear bearings supports the spindle assembly, on which the arms are mounted. The entire assembly is moved by the Z-motor thereby maintaining a constant load throughout the Z travel. The Z motor incorporates a brake that engages after prolonged inactivity on the Z-axis or in the case of an EMO. All motor drives assemblies have hard-limit stops, and each drive is protected by over-travel limitations set in software.
